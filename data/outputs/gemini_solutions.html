(i) Definition of Simple Harmonic Motion

Simple harmonic motion (SHM) is a periodic motion in which the restoring force is directly proportional to the displacement from the equilibrium position and acts in the opposite direction.

(ii) Derivation of Time Period for a Mass-Spring System

For a mass m attached to a vertical spring of force constant k, the equation of motion is:

\[m\frac{d^2x}{dt^2} = -kx\]

where x is the displacement from the equilibrium position.

The solution to this equation is:

\[x(t) = A\cos(\omega t + \phi)\]

where A is the amplitude, ω is the angular frequency, and ϕ is the phase angle.

The time period T is the time taken for one complete oscillation, which is given by:

\[T = \frac{2\pi}{\omega}\]

Substituting the angular frequency from the equation of motion, we get:

\[T = 2\pi\sqrt{\frac{m}{k}}\]

(iii) Time Period Inside a Satellite

Inside a satellite, the system is in free fall and experiences weightlessness. Therefore, the restoring force is not due to gravity but due to the spring force. The equation of motion becomes:

\[m\frac{d^2x}{dt^2} = -kx\]

The time period is still given by:

\[T = 2\pi\sqrt{\frac{m}{k}}\]
